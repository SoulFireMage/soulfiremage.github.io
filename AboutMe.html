About Me

Programming, AI, Maths, Blender and Writing

I started learning to program in 2010; the context was to help a small company I was working for develop their product. This was never officially my role, but code I researched and wrote for them exists in the product today. Since then at various times I've tackled more of the .Net world: VB, C# and F#. In addition I added SQL and R skills at various times. A later role built on these.

Prior to all this, I'd a few years experience in general IT but I spent a lot of time learning completely different fields: neuroscience and hypnotherapy to name a couple. In February 2015 I began teaching myself to model in Blender - a fantastic piece of open source software. This is where the strange looking ship/creatures on my pages come from.

Machine learning and AI fascinates me - this is an area I plan to delve deeply into over the next decade or more.

Woven throughout the last 18 months or more has been the task of learning some higher mathematics. My starting point was a GCSE education, so there was (still is) a leap from basics to the higher level conceptualisation required to tackle this area. However, to really understand how AI works, I feel it's an area I'll be constantly coming back to for decades. At time of writing, I do see it as a science of patterns of relationships.

My formal education stopped at 16 - however since then I've spent much of my time as an auto didact; with the modern internet, I believe hard smart work can replace spending thousands on formal education in many circumstances.

The writing and the starships: Their world is an idea I had in November 2014 and began with sketches of the characters first. I tried to plot a story arc with them, nearly killed the whole thing for me. I'm an extrapolator more than a planner, at least with creative pursuits and have since realised their story will grow over time rather than be rushed out to fill a plot. The ships are described more fully on their website, though at time of writing I need to rebuild the site with a more experience eye to design!

January 2016: I'm currently between roles and actively seeking new work. My site aims to demonstrate via my repositories and web pages that since I began programming, I've learnt to be adaptable.
