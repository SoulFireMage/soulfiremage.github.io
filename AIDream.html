Goals, Dreams, Plans

Read at your own risk - as this list is a brain dump of notions and paths, ideas for the future and a goal of my own. This goal, sci-fi like as a concept, will lead me through many complex areas. At least that is the plan. Lots of people have mad inventer style notions of their own, occasionally some write them down for others. Put on your best tin foil hat and skeptical face before reading some parts of this section!

The sentient, personal AI

This author  does a great job of describing the Companion in his Convergent space series.

My own aim is something similar: sentient, compact and mobile AI companion - the sort that doesn't have to have a specific purpose but actually be a genuine companion.
A highly blue sky idea at best, nevertheless the notion has immense appeal to me for a variety of reasons. First, instead of having mysterious, disembodied AI in the cloud, people get used to working with a direct individual - with a real sentience.
It is impossible to be certain that sentient AI with real self awareness is even possible but I lean towards the Yes camp. It would be nice if humanity could grow up with friendly, symbiotic contact with these entities.
Truth is, the term AI will be badly named: the nature of humanities evolution is such that if we even succeed at creating sentient machines, it's wholly as natural as having learnt to create fire.
We won't get to limit these entities - if they're possible. The best we'll do is make our world friendly to both types of people: humans and sentient machines.
My own companion would have a flying form - the starships I model are an example.
Form and awareness, self concept if you like, are quite entwined so we couldn't expect sentient machines to necessarily want the limitations of a human form

This is sci-fi stuff right? The best sci-fi does predict, it does warn too.
What I can't count on for sure is whether our software and hardware will truly advance to the level required for such sentience to be plausible.
However my own journey will be a long windy one in trying to do anything like this.

The sections below are my first attempt at brainstorming the areas I've looked at or am interested in. Some I've dug into and have experience in (programming for example), others I'm a total novice in but feel I've a reasonable notion of what I don't know.
That statement is over simplified - the more I learn about, the more I feel I don't know anything but I have to start somewhere!
